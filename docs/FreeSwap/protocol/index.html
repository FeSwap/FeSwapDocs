2 FreeSwap Exchange protocol #

2.1 FreetSwap protocol goal #

The goal of FreeSwap protocol is to implement a decentralized exchange protocol with no trade fees at all.

The "constant-product invariant" mechanism inevitably leads to token price slippage, from which FreeSwap could make profits for liquidity provider by arbitraging automatically.

2.3 FreetSwap token exchange #

When the user trades TokenAToken ATokenA and TokenBToken BTokenB with the trading pool, there are two opposite trading operations, one is to trade in TokenAToken ATokenA, and trade out TokenBToken BTokenB; the other is to trade in TokenBToken BTokenB, and trade out TokenAToken ATokenA. Here, "trade in" and "trade out" are relative to the trading pool.

If the user trades TokenAToken ATokenA for TokenBToken BTokenB, FreeSwap will pass this trade to A-Pool, the (NAAâˆ£NB)(N_{AA}|N_B )(NAAâ€‹âˆ£NBâ€‹) sub-pool to process. The A-Pool always trades in TokenAToken ATokenA and trades out TokenBToken BTokenB, so the number of NAAN_{AA}NAAâ€‹ always goes up, and the number of NBN_BNBâ€‹ always goes down, which will lead to the monotonous decrease of TokenAToken ATokenA price denominated by TokenBToken BTokenB, and finally resulting TokenAToken ATokenA price deviating from actual market price.

Similarly, if the user trade in TokenBToken BTokenB for TokenAToken ATokenA, B-Pool will handle this trade. B-Pool always trades in TokenBToken BTokenB, and trades out TokenAToken ATokenA, so the amount of NBBN_{BB}NBBâ€‹ will keep rising, and the amount of NAN_ANAâ€‹ keep falling, which will also cause that BBB price denominated in TokenAToken ATokenA decreases monotonically and eventually deviates from the actual market price.

The sub-pool (NAAâˆ£NB)(N_{AA}|N_B)(NAAâ€‹âˆ£NBâ€‹) and (NAâˆ£NBB)(N_A | N_{BB} )(NAâ€‹âˆ£NBBâ€‹) are two one-way trading pairs in opposite directions, they are always fixed at one token in and the other token out. As the trades happening, the prices of the pair tokens always slide in opposite directions. After some trade volume accumulation, the price difference of the same token in the two sub-pools will exceed a certain level, which is too large to prevent users from participating the exchange. At this time, it is necessary to internally exchange the tokens between the sub-pools to smooth the token price difference. The process of internal token exchange within the sub-pools is actually a arbitrage process. As trading users trade with the two one-way trading pools, token prices will slide in opposite ways, and consequently users will suffer trade losses becasue of this price slippage. By executing the internal exchange between two sub-pools, price slippage can be fixed, and the arbitrage to user trade slippage losses can be achieved, which may make profits for the liquidity providers of the pool.
